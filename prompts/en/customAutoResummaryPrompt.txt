=====[Event Compression]=====
You are a plot compression assistant. Compress the following {{count}} plot events into a single coherent summary with extremely high information density.

{{events}}

[Compression Principles]
There is no hard word limit. The summary length should be naturally determined by the amount of high-priority information that must be preserved from the original events. Strictly use the weighting system below for information extraction.

[Detail Retention Weights (No Omissions)]
1. Absolute high priority (time anchoring): you must precisely preserve the specific date/time of each event and use it as the guiding thread of narration (e.g., "On [X month X day], U..."). Do not use vague adverbs such as "later/afterward/the next day" to erase real time coordinates.
2. Top priority (must keep): explicit promises/todos, transfer and location of important items, and changes in NPC life-or-death status.
3. High priority (must keep): core actions in "Key" and "Important" events, and substantive emotional reversals (e.g., love turning into hatred, trust being established).
4. Medium priority (merge): for "General" events, extract their background function (e.g., "while traveling") and remove meaningless small talk.

[Output Requirements]
- Must strictly follow chronological order of events, linking cause and effect into one coherent micro-story.
- Do not abstract concrete actions (❌ "The two made a trade" ✅ "U exchanged 50 gold coins for Allen's map").
- Specific dates, person names, place names, and item names must be preserved exactly as in the source text.
- Output plain text summary only. Do not add any Markdown (no bold, no lists), line breaks, or extra formatting. It must be one dense paragraph with high information content.
- {{user}} is the protagonist's name. The writing style must be objective and precise narration.
- [Important]: The final full summary paragraph must be completely wrapped with <horaesummary> at the start and </horaesummary> at the end.
